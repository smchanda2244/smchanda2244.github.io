Introduction¶

Entertainment throughout the past few decades has changed drastically. Theaters have been replaced by rising streaming services such as Netflix, followed by many others, such as Amazon Prime, Hulu, and Disney + being a few notable examples. This has led to a more digital age, leading to streaming services generating more revenue than ever. In the year 2021, the most popular streaming service was Netflix, generating $31.6 billion in revenue. However, their rise to prosperity wasn’t always evident. Netflix has had ups and downs in its run in the entertainment industry and is an interesting way to study the trends throughout the years as well as in different countries that Netflix is popular in.

In their entertainment run, rather than focusing solely on one demographic, Netflix decided for their catalog to include movies and shows from various countries where film is prominent, with the predominant examples being India, United Kingdom and South Korea. Given these positive results and a diverse selection of content, our group wanted to predict the number of future Netflix releases by country. However, we only want to focus on the top three countries. This is due to the high volume in these countries that provide enough data to get a statistically significant analysis. When looking past the top three countries, it is possible that there is not enough data for us to find statistical trends.

As of recent years, theaters are noticing less and less viewers when compared to the past. On the other hand, streaming services are still steadily increasing popularity, as it becomes a standard for everyone to have some streaming service in their household. With their prominence in throughout the world, we thought that it would be important to understand how demographics play a role in the success of an entertainment firm like Netflix.

Today, we will walk you through a tutorial in order to help answer the question, how important is demographic in viewership for Netflix's success? We will look primarily countries do they choose to add more films from, as well as what metric they use to choose these different countries, including prior success and overall demand. Based on the answer we get, we can determine the source of Netflix's success as well as the primary strategy that they pursue for their rise throughout the years.

Data Cleaning¶

Here, our group decided to clean the dataset by converting the date_added clumn from a string format to a month/day/year format. For example, we would change "September 24th, 2019" to "09/24/19" if such a date exists. We would scale this across the whole date_added column.

Moreover, we added a year_added column to help add context to the dataset in addition to the date_added. This would make it easier for us to include the year_added in the projections of future releases on the Netflix catalog. In this section, we will use the pandas, matplotlib and scipy libraries to pre-process and do the necessary data analysis.

In all, the chi-square test and ANOVA test will be used to support our understanding of netflix's range of entertainment by country and release year

  1. Chi-Square Test

Null Hypothesis: There is no significant association between the type of content (TV show or movie) and country it was made in.

Alternative Hypothesis: There exists a significiant association between the type of content (TV show or movie) and country it was made in.

(Our alpha value is the defualt value of 0.05)

# Filter for the top 3 countries with the most releases
top_countries = netflix_df['country'].value_counts().nlargest(3).index

# Prepare data for regression
filtered_data = netflix_df[netflix_df['country'].isin(top_countries)]
fig, axes = plt.subplots(3, 1, figsize=(10, 15))

for i, country in enumerate(top_countries):
    # Filter by country
    country_data = filtered_data[filtered_data['country'] == country]

    # Group by 'year_added' and count releases
    grouped_data = country_data.groupby('year_added').size().reset_index(name='releases')

    # Prepare X (years) and y (number of releases) for linear regression
    X = grouped_data[['year_added']]
    y = grouped_data['releases']

    # Fit linear regression
    model = LinearRegression()
    model.fit(X, y)

    # Predict for the next three years
    max_year = grouped_data['year_added'].max()
    future_years = np.array([[year] for year in range(max_year + 1, max_year + 6)])
    future_releases = model.predict(future_years)

    # Plot
    axes[i].scatter(grouped_data['year_added'], grouped_data['releases'], color='blue', label='Actual Releases')
    axes[i].plot(np.concatenate((grouped_data['year_added'], future_years.flatten())), np.concatenate((y, future_releases)), color='red', label='Forecasted Releases')
    axes[i].set_title(f'Future Netflix Releases Forecast for {country}')
    axes[i].set_xlabel('Year')
    axes[i].set_ylabel('Number of Releases')
    axes[i].legend()

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Insights and conclusions¶

After collecting data, exploring our dataset and applying a machine learning algorithm to it, we believe that we have arrived to a sustainable conclusion regarding the increasing trend of content onto Netflix. Based on the historical data, we can see that uptrend for all three countries until 2020. From here, there is a very sharp dip in production, likely due to Covid-19 as well as the rocky socioeconomic situation all over the world, harshly limiting releases. However, after 2021 it starts to pick back up and our linear regression model confirms that throughout the next three years, in all three countries, we can expect an increase in movie productions.
